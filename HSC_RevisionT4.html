Topic 4: Roles of Organisations

Comprehensive Revision Hub for CCEA Unit 3. Master the highly specific roles of Statutory, Voluntary, Private Organisations, Private Practitioners, and International bodies.

Statutory Sector

Services that are paid for and provided by the government (e.g., NHS, school nursing, social services). They are legally obligated to provide free-at-the-point-of-use care based on public need.

Voluntary Sector

Services provided by non-profit making organisations that are often registered charities. They rely heavily on public donations, fundraising, and volunteers to operate and fill gaps in statutory care.

Private Sector

Organisations and independent practitioners that are run as a business in order to make a profit. Service users generally pay directly out-of-pocket, via private health insurance, or via government subcontracts.
